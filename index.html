Straight Stitch Sewing Machines

A sewing machine stitches fabric together using a needle and thread working together in an up-and-down motion. While early sewing machines were powered manually—usually with the operator spinning a wheel—modern sewing machines are powered by electricity, and operators press a foot pedal to determine the stitching speed.

Whether you’ve got plans to become an expert quilter or just want to work through a few basic sewing patterns, sewing can be a fun and rewarding hobby; however, it can also seem daunting in the beginning. Before you make your first stitch, learn the basics, including how to use a sewing machine and set it up properly.
